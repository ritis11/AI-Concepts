The law of large numbers is a probability theorem stating that as a sample size increases, the average of observed results approaches the expected theoretical value. It indicates that while small samples may deviate, large numbers of independent trials produce stable long-term results.Key Aspects of the Law of Large NumbersSample Average Convergence: As the number of trials (\(n\)) increases, the average \(\overline{X}\) of the results moves closer to the expected value \(E[X]\).Two Main Versions:Weak Law (WLLN): States that the sample mean converges to the expected value in probability, meaning for a large \(n\), the probability that the average differs from the mean by more than a small amount is small.Strong Law (SLLN): States that the sample mean converges to the expected value "almost surely" or with probability 1.
